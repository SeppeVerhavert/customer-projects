WORK/LIFE BALANCE: $190 Billion dollars. According to new Stanford research, that is how much workplace stress - long hours, job insecurity and lack of work/life balance - is costing companies.

Although many companies institute wellness programs, such a smoking cessation, yoga and other mindfulness programs, there is a conspicuous inattention to the work environment itself. This connects directly with a culture of psychological safety and belonging.

Without those foundational aspects of work, the stress and burnout are more than likely. Although many companies have impressive parental leave and other benefits, some of these companies have a culture where there is an implicit understanding that taking that time will cost your career advancement. Combine these "ghost benefits" with a psychological unsafe culture and it's a recipe for churn, low morale, decreased performance and flat revenue growth.

Attracting high-performers back to work and retaining them will require a commitment from companies to provide family-friendly benefits and policies to encourage their use.

IN PRACTICE: Here are some practices and behaviors that are consistent with excellent work/life balance oriented companies to consider when addressing gaps:

  • Flexible work options
  • Executives model by conspicuously practicing work/life balance
  • Enforced "off grid" boundaries for employees regarding tech and work related communication
  • Managers help balance performance with home responsibilities for team members
  • Senior leaders and supervisors are visible and vocal about protecting mental and physical wellbeing

KPI HIGHLIGHTS:

  1. Pulse or MBI assessment shows a company wide burnout rate of less than 50%
  2. Participation rates in benefit packages are over 50%
  3. Employee take-up rate for individual benefits (where applicable) are above 70% [Bureau of Labor Statistics]
  4. Absenteeism is less than national average of 2.8% [2018 Bureau of Labor Statistics]
