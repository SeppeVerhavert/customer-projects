The good…

  • You are structured and precise.
  • You follow well established rules and procedures in an exacting fashion and find comfort in boundaries and order.
  • You tend to discover solutions through research and planning, exhausting all possible options and moving forward with confidence. These are your strongest qualities and should be leveraged heavily to create optimal performance for yourself and your team.

The not so good…

  • You can sometimes be hyper analytical, which leads you to spending too much time on irrelevant details.
  • Your work tends to take a long time and you struggle with bouts of paralysis by analysis, which throttles your productivity.
  • You are exacting, sometimes to a fault, and this can hamper your potential as a team member. These are the traits you should be aware of and try to keep in check when possible.

The company you should keep…

  • Cartographers work great in teams.
  • You can compile an unlimited number of cartographers and it will increase your productivity accordingly. But it is important to note that cartographers tend to benefit exponentially from solid leadership. need someone to push them into action.
  • A team of cartographers is an excellent choice for precision in execution, but for optimum productivity, it’s best to have a trailblazer or some explorers on the team as well. This will help maintain a healthy balance. With all of the careful deliberation from making maps, it’s best to make sure there is aggressive leadership from a trailblazer or a handful of explorers. This will help maintain a healthy balance between the high level of execution and expediency.
  • Allowing someone to “dirty up” your tidy workspace a bit may drive you a little crazy from time to time, but you will get more done this way, which will ultimately make your efforts more productive and effective.
  • One trailblazer or a small number of explorers are a great complement to cartographers, who reside on one end of the spectrum
