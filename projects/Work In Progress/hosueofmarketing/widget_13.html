Branding & Positioning

The strongest brands have the power to inspire and shape the market.
Creating your brand and building a strong positioning in the market all starts with the raison d’être of your business.

Why do you exist as a company? What added value do you deliver to the market? What makes you stand out in today’s continuous evolving environment?
 
Value Proposition - What is it?

A Value Proposition isn't just the product or service you agree to deliver to the customer: it's the ingredient of your business that solves a problem competitors can't.

Definition 
A value proposition is a promise of value to be delivered. It’s the reason a prospect should buy from you (and not from your competitor)




Attributes of a good value proposition
… Define what you do
… Explain how your product resolves (a) pain point(s) for you potential customer
… Be short, specific and easy to understand. In other words: be crystal clear 
… Be displayed prominently on your website and/or your customer touch points
… Answer the question: ‘if I am your ideal customer, why should I buy from you instead of any of your competitors?’

 


STEP 1: Why does your brand exist? 

Before launching a new brand on the market, a key question we need to answer is: why should your brand exist? A strong brand adds value to the market and remains relevant in an ever-changing environment. Therefore, we need to understand what the drivers of the (people behind the) brand are, what it stands for and why consumers should believe in your company or brand purpose. If this is unclear, we recommend to start with the basics and make sure your why is well defined and relevant.


STEP 2: Understand your customer needs! 

In order to become or remain a strong brand,  we should always start with the customer in mind. Before a brand can be (re)built, we need to gain a clear and deep understanding of the unmet needs of the (target) customer , as well as the opportunities to be found within the market. Listening to customers and prospects, in order to understand their wishes, experiences and struggles, is key to building your brand.

You should then use these insights to create a persona. A customer or prospect persona helps you to put yourself into the shoes of the people that could buy your product or services. It allows you to know who they are, what needs they have, what context they live in, and ultimately personas will help shape your offering in order to provide relevant value that customers will acknowledge you for. Creating fictional characters and zooming in on their everyday activities, could also help you discover their unmet needs. And this might just be the silver bullet you need to outsmart your competition.

Definition 

Personas are fictional customer profiles, developed as a way of representing a particular group based on their shared interests. They represent a ‘character’ with which your team can engage. Personas consolidate descriptions of user behavior patterns into representative profiles, to humanize design focus, test scenarios, and aid communication.


Why should you use personas?

  • Helps you put yourself into your user’s shoes to know who they are
  • Help you understand (real and potential) customers better
  • Helps you humanize design focus, test scenarios and aid communication
  • They provide a shared understanding of customers across your organization and a valuable basis for journey maps
  • To define and engage the different interest-groups that may exist within your target segment
